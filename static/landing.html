What Happened

The Night Ahoro-Esinele Was Attacked

In the early hours of Friday, 16 May 2026, armed gunmen descended on two communities in Oriire Local Government Area — Ahoro-Esinele and Yawota — targeting their schools.

The attackers arrived on motorcycles, dressed in military-style uniforms. Residents initially believed they were security operatives. Moments later, gunfire shattered the calm of the night.

The gunmen moved swiftly from classroom to classroom at Community High School, Ahoro-Esinele and Yawota Baptist Nursery and Primary School, loading victims onto motorcycles before disappearing into the dense forest reserve of the Old Oyo National Park.

A mathematics teacher, Mr. Michael Oyedokun, was killed in captivity. The school principal, Mrs. Rachael Folawe Alamu, was driven away in her own vehicle. The youngest known victim is a 2-year-old girl, Christianah Akanbi.

The communities sit 42 km from Ogbomosho, surrounded by forest, with poor roads, no social amenities, and minimal internet access — conditions that delayed help reaching them.
